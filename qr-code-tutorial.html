ü§î What Are QR Codes?

QR (Quick Response) codes are two-dimensional barcodes that can store various types of information and be scanned by smartphone cameras. Unlike traditional barcodes, QR codes can hold much more data and are readable from any direction.

üí° Did You Know? QR codes were invented in 1994 by a Japanese company for tracking automotive parts. Today, they're used worldwide for everything from marketing to payments.

üèÜ Best Practices & Pro Tips

Design Guidelines

  • Test Before Printing: Always scan with multiple phone cameras
  • Size Matters: Minimum 2√ó2 cm for print, larger for distance scanning
  • High Contrast: Dark QR on light background works best
  • Quiet Zone: Leave blank space around QR code (minimum 4 modules)

Optimization Tips

  • Use URL shorteners for cleaner codes and tracking
  • Add a call-to-action near the QR code ("Scan to visit")
  • Place at eye level (120-150cm from ground)
  • Ensure good lighting for scanning

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • ‚ùå Placing QR codes on curved surfaces
  • ‚ùå Using reflective materials
  • ‚ùå Making QR codes too small
  • ‚ùå Linking to non-mobile-friendly websites
  • ‚ùå Not testing on different devices

Are QR codes from your tool free forever? +

Yes! All QR codes generated with our tool are 100% free with no expiration. No watermarks, no subscriptions, no hidden fees.

Can I edit a QR code after creating it? +

Static QR codes (like the ones our tool creates) cannot be edited after generation. However, you can generate a new one in seconds with updated content. For editable QR codes, you would need a dynamic QR code service (usually paid).

What's the difference between static and dynamic QR codes? +

Static QR Codes: Content is fixed, cannot be changed after creation. Best for permanent links or information.

Dynamic QR Codes: Content can be updated without changing the QR code image. Usually requires a paid service. Best for marketing campaigns where links might change.

Do QR codes have scanning limits? +

No, there are no scanning limits. A QR code can be scanned unlimited times. The only limitation is the durability of the material it's printed on!

Can QR codes contain viruses or malware? +

QR codes themselves are just images and cannot contain viruses. However, they can link to malicious websites. Always check the URL preview on your phone before visiting. Our tool shows exactly what your QR code contains before you generate it.
